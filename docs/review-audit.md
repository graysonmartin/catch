# App Review Guidelines Audit

Audit of Catch against Apple's App Review Guidelines, performed March 4, 2026.

---

## 1. Safety

### 1.1 Objectionable Content
- **Status**: PASS
- The app contains user-generated content (photos of cats, text notes, comments). Social features (comments/likes) are limited to users who sign in with Apple. No objectionable content is generated by the app itself.
- **Action needed**: Consider adding a mechanism to report inappropriate content in comments/social features before submission. Apple may ask about content moderation for user-generated content (Guideline 1.2).

### 1.2 User-Generated Content
- **Status**: NEEDS REVIEW
- The app has social features (comments, likes, follows). Apple requires apps with user-generated content to include:
  - A method for filtering objectionable material
  - A mechanism for users to report offensive content
  - The ability to block abusive users
  - Published contact information for users to reach the developer
- **Action needed**: Verify that a report/block mechanism exists, or add one before submission. This is a common rejection reason.

### 1.3 Kids Category
- **Status**: N/A
- The app is not targeted at children and does not claim to be in the Kids category.

## 5. Legal

### 5.1 Privacy

#### 5.1.1 Data Collection and Storage
- **Status**: PASS
- Privacy policy created and hosted at `docs/privacy-policy.html`.
- The app collects: location (when in use), photos (user-selected), cat encounter data, Apple Sign In credentials, social data (follows/likes/comments).
- All data stored on-device via SwiftData or synced via Apple CloudKit. No third-party servers.

#### 5.1.2 App Privacy Details (Nutrition Label)
- **Status**: ACTION NEEDED
- When submitting to App Store Connect, the privacy nutrition label must be filled out. Relevant data types:
  - **Location**: When In Use — used for app functionality (tagging encounters)
  - **Photos**: Selected photos only — used for app functionality
  - **User ID**: Apple Sign In identifier — used for app functionality and social features
  - **Other Data**: Cat names, notes, encounter details — used for app functionality
- None of this data is used for tracking. None is shared with third parties.

#### 5.1.3 Required Info.plist Keys
| Key | Value | Status |
|-----|-------|--------|
| `NSLocationWhenInUseUsageDescription` | "Catch uses your location to record where you spotted a cat." | PRESENT |
| `NSPhotoLibraryUsageDescription` | "Catch needs access to your photo library to add photos of cats." | PRESENT |
| `NSCameraUsageDescription` | N/A (camera not used) | NOT NEEDED |

- **Status**: PASS. Both required keys are present with clear, user-friendly descriptions that explain why the permission is needed.

### 5.2 Sign in with Apple

- **Status**: PASS (with note)
- The app uses Sign in with Apple for authentication.
- Apple requires that apps using Sign in with Apple must offer account deletion (Guideline 5.1.1(v)).
- **Account deletion is implemented** in `SettingsView.swift` under the "Danger Zone" section. Users can delete their account, which removes local data and signs them out.
- **Note**: Ensure the account deletion also handles CloudKit data cleanup. Apple may test that deleting an account actually removes user data from the cloud, not just the local device.

### 5.3 Intellectual Property
- **Status**: PASS
- No third-party IP used. App icon is original. No copyrighted imagery.

## 7. Potential Rejection Risks

### High Priority
1. **User-generated content moderation** (Guideline 1.2) — The app has social features (comments, likes, follows) but may lack report/block functionality. Apple will likely ask about content moderation. Add report and block mechanisms if not already present.

2. **CloudKit data deletion** (Guideline 5.1.1(v)) — The account deletion flow removes local data but should also clean up CloudKit records (user profile, social data). Apple may verify this during review.

### Medium Priority
3. **Privacy nutrition label accuracy** — Must be filled out correctly in App Store Connect. Inaccurate labels are a common rejection cause.

4. **IPv6 compatibility** — Apple requires all apps to work on IPv6-only networks. CloudKit and standard Apple frameworks handle this, but worth verifying if any custom networking is added later.

### Low Priority
5. **Launch screen** — The auto-generated launch screen works but is plain white. Not a rejection risk, but a polish opportunity.

6. **App Store screenshots** — Not a code issue, but required for submission. Need screenshots for all required device sizes (6.7", 6.1", and optionally iPad).
